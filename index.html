Honest Answers

Questions I get on every first call.

Why $497? That seems low for what's included.

Because the audit is the filter, not the product. The real revenue is in the sprint and retainer. $497 is engineered to filter tire-kickers without scaring serious buyers — the typical "AI audit" runs $2,000–$3,000 and many are vapor. If you can't justify $497 to find leaks in your business, we shouldn't work together yet.

How is this different from an AI agency?

Three things. One: I run my own e-commerce brand, my own SaaS, my own routing API, my own trading research platform — I'm not selling a service I've never used. Two: every engagement starts with a paid audit, so I'm scoping against your real data. Three: I ship in 5 days, not 5 months. If you want a 30-page strategy deck, hire an agency. If you want a system live this week, hire me.

What if I don't have a clear AI problem to solve?

That's exactly what the audit is for. Most buyers don't have an "AI problem" — they have a leverage problem, a workflow problem, or a data problem that AI happens to be the right tool for. The 5-lens audit finds the highest-leverage fixes whether they involve AI or not. I'll tell you what to not build with AI as often as I'll tell you what to build.

Who owns the code and the work product?

You do, once you've paid in full. I retain the right to use anonymized methodology, scorecards, audit templates, and reusable code patterns — your data, your specific implementations, and your branded deliverables are yours. The contract spells this out cleanly.

Do you offer revenue share?

Sometimes. Only after we've worked together on at least a sprint, only when your reporting is clean enough to measure incremental revenue honestly, and only with a written addendum that locks the baseline at signing. 3–7% of incremental revenue, capped, 6–12 month term. It's the exception, not the default.

Do you sign NDAs?

Yes, mutual NDAs are standard before any deep-dive call. One-year confidentiality, standard carve-outs, Florida governing law. I'll send you one or sign yours.

What if my industry is regulated — healthcare, legal, finance?

I work with vendors and operators adjacent to regulated industries (insurance agencies, healthcare suppliers, accounting firms) but I do not provide legal, medical, or financial advice and I don't touch protected health information. If your work product is the regulated thing itself, I'm probably not the right fit. The audit will tell us either way.

Can I see past client work?

I'm intentionally honest: I'm taking on my first cohort of external clients now. My proof is the portfolio above — projects I've built, shipped, and operated myself. The first three clients to sign get a discounted sprint in exchange for being a case study. The audit itself tells you whether my pattern-matching translates to your business.

What's the refund policy on the audit?

Full refund if I fail to deliver the audit and Loom within 5 business days of payment + completed intake. Otherwise non-refundable once the audit is delivered. Full terms in the audit agreement.

How do I actually pay?

Right now: Zelle, wire transfer, or Venmo — your choice. After you submit intake, I review and send payment instructions by email. The audit clock starts when payment is confirmed. Card/Stripe checkout coming soon.
